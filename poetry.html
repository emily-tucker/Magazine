Cardinal Red

Tessa Lindahl

Can’t you see the noises and tell me what color the word Thursday is?
Its green.
An emerald green.
I don’t like Thursdays but the color I can deal with.
Mondays are nasty and yellow but not the kind of yellow that little kids color the sun to be or sunflower yellow or corn yellow or even road paint yellow.
Its a mustard color.
Like the gas, not the condiment.
It’s ugly and frustrating.

Some people have colors in their names.
My mother’s is a lilac color.
My brother’s is the color of a salmon’s belly.
My best friend has an E in his name that is always purple.
But there is one name that comes to mind that I don’t like.
It’s a cardinal red that is beautiful and entrancing and almost as obnoxious as an ugly Christmas sweater and stoic and happy and angry and monumental.
It’s not that I don’t like the color, it’s that I have feelings attached to that certain name and now that certain hue that I will never get out of my head or out of my life and I will see it every day for as long as I remember because it is as unforgettable as soaking in gasoline fumes and stars.

If I try really hard, I don’t always see the colors of the words.
I can be normal for split seconds of time but then I go back to seeing the mess of everything in my head.
Cardinal red used me feel normal.
He made me feel like the blood coursing through my veins wasn’t candy colored and my mind wasn’t painted with abominable stripes.
And I hate cardinal red now because the name attached to it showed me that normality for me will always be an illusion.
An illusion filled with mustard colored Mondays and emerald Thursdays and purple colored E’s that don’t make sense because I know that all of these words are written with black ink and only I can see the fucking colors that don’t mix well and turn into a botched piece of brown art when they get jumbled.
Cardinal red has completely fucked me over.
It’s a good thing not many people have that color in their name.



Light and Dark

Donielle Carr

It’s like when you’re standing on a precipice,
And the clouds appear dark and foreboding.
Cold sweeps up through the valley,
And an icy chill clutches your chest.

The view is spectacular,
The feeling mute.
A stream of light bursts forth from cloud,
Illuminating the majestic Red Lady.

There is a veracity in its light,
A beauty that beckons.

For when you are in the light, it is blinding,
The darkness becomes consumed and appears to be no more.
When you are in the light, it is everything,
Your being becomes enveloped by the warm glow that draws you in.

But the sun shall not shine forever,
Balance is needed, an accord between the light and dark.

Where there was once a brilliant streak of sunshine,
There is now only shadow.
Though it is shadow that implies the sun still remains,
Merely hidden from sight.

I turn my back on the darkness enfolding,
Only to realize that I have it surrounding,
And that it too has a blinding effect.

I walk on.

Within certain glances
There are sunspots still visible before my eyes,
A transparency over the gloomy shade.
Haunting, like a memory.

It keeps you going while holding you back,
As these sunspots are remnants,
Visions of the past.

Look to the future,
Where the sun will rise again.

Desolation Canyon

Anna Boyle

Coffee digs between my teeth grounds grinding
A corpse within my sleeping bag,
I rise from the morning, mummified,
confined to a transient dawn
The light soft, settled
rising above timberline.
Dew sticks along morning
condensation between breaths
between breaths, breathing.

Kepler 16bS

Donielle Carr

The stark arid landscape expands beneath
The horizon with its light twice as bright,
As two fading suns cast a blood orange sky.
The binary beauty of a system
Explored by an extrasolar planet.

Heat meets the cool terrestrial surface.
A thin veil of fog sweeps the ground below,

Glowing in a mysterious matter.

In my mind, we are not light-years apart.

Midnight Vanilla with Light Moisturizers

Frederick Slyter

As life evolves,
more and more
of my dreams
die. I now know
I’ll never be
a knight, a wizard,
a time-traveler.
I won’t visit other planets,
speak to animals,
or fly unassisted.
To cope, to deal,
to drink these sorrows
away, another dream
dies. I cannot find
a quiet pub,
a dark Blues room
or a cocktail café
That hasn’t been modernized
into a smoke-free, spit-free, McCafé
without sawdust
on the floor
or peanut shells
on the bar.
Every time I leave
the men’s room
my hands smell
like Midnight Vanilla
With Light Moisturizers.
This is exactly
what’s wrong with the world today.



November

Holly Rios

Every year, Ferlinghetti asks me,
“Who are we now?”
I still do not know how to answer that.
Mostly because I still do not know.
Every year, late fall, in the crisp cool decay, I remember
people getting divorced.
I ask,
Ferlinghetti, did you ever split your own seams?
Or did you remain intact?
Late November at age 19
I drank vodka in the Minneapolis cold
with a homeless stranger
who remorsefully
offered me the bottle at a bus stop
as if it were rude to drink without
me.
And I wanted to ask him,
Do you think that people can bust themselves at the seams?
Do you think that people can sew themselves back together?
On that Thursday, I drank too much white wine,
and contemplated Ferlinghetti.
I wonder if I ought to resole my boots.
I wonder if I should try to fix what is already there,
though it’s worn down.
Wine hazed, wondering,
if you still think I feel like home
when it’s hard for me to even hold a conversation or
remember to brush my teeth.
I wonder if

you still want to be my heel, my traction.



People Are As So

Marlena Romero

There stands a boy
and there stands a girl.
There stands a phrase:
“What did I do to deserve
you?” A bad boy beckons
a good girl giddy with glee.

He locks her eyes in the tinted
windows of his soul, tickling
her yearning of the words she dreams
of hearing. The phrase is voiced.

She locks his eyes and her
windows begin to tint a blind-
sided black. The good girl
is grungy, deceiving, light
out for people to keep
from judging what is dark.
The bad boy is beautiful, bodacious,
a tough attitude to keep
people from seeing just how much
he does care.

Bad karma to bad people
and good karma to the good. Bad
people are bad karma and good
people keep secrets.
Good people are good karma
and bad people want
to be burned.

Hopeless are the secrets, useful
are the lies, dangerous
are the truths, all held below.



Sleep Paralysis

Mason Brainard

A jolt of electricity rushes through my body like an ocean wave, my bones feel heavy, full of lead. I gasp for breath as cold pale hands squeeze tighter around my neck. Everything is black, it is as if I am floating in an abyss of endless nothingness. It feels like I’ve been drugged: my head spins, my eyes water, my nerves tingle. When I finally open my eyes a knot of fire explodes in my chest as I realize, my attacker, this monster, is me.

She has MY face, MY body, MY hands that are around MY neck. But her eyes are not mine, they are not hazel with hints of green and gold, they are black lifeless pits staring back at me. Laughing, knowing that she’s got the best of me, that she is winning.

I grab the hands around my throat and pull, kick, scratch, anything to get her off of me, to breathe, to escape this evil.

Why is she doing this to me?
Who is this devil version of myself?
Is this the real me…?

And if it is, why is she stronger than me? More powerful, more motivated, more beautiful than I could ever dream to be. She is killing me. Taking over every hair, every freckle, every molecule that is me. And filling them with her.

I am about to lose it. The oxygen leaves my lungs, black spots cloud the edges of my vision, and my own hands go limp. She has won.

Then I feel my eyes open, the black spots clear, I take violent deep breaths, trying to regain the feeling in my body. I look in the mirror and see my hazel green eyes again. But something is different, I almost miss the girl I saw a few moments ago, I almost want her back.

Telamon

Madison Manning

Time is now incomprehensible
Nonexistent is mercy for mistakes
Perpetual struggle, punishment for battle
Various pressures and strains, source the anxiety
The excruciating pain builds between the sculpted blades
Numbed by an unbearable ache, external, internal, agony eternal

The Box in the Attic

Mason Brainard

I walk up the creaking stairs, dust
filling my lungs. There are wardrobes
and trunks, and pictures of dead guys,
their eyes follow my every move.

A box catches my baby toe, arousing
my curiosity. The box is old, but it comes alive
in my hands, begging to be opened.

Cold air burns my eyes, revealing
a hunting jacket and a handgun.
I know there is a secret here,

one that I
am not supposed to know.

The box is evil. Filled to the tipping point of
sin.

A photograph of a couple
of young killers smiling runs chills down my back.
They are happy, laughing with no remorse for
what they’ve done.

Old wives tales creep into my memory,
they are why my grandmother could not go out at night
when she was young.

The boy is long gone, shot between
the shoulder blades, in a dark empty lot,
somewhere in Virginia. His dark curls matted
with dirt.

The gun is rusty, I feel its power
as I hold it against my heart,
calling me to his unfinished business.

Don’t tell my grandmother…
but he likes me. Tells me
I’m beautiful,

he says he’ll show me how to use
the gun.



The Sky Beyond Carsosa

Jazz Pool

The time came
when my journeys
surrendered me to Carcosa.

That timeless city,
where black stars
gleamed above
nightshade meadows,
and fragrant flowers
bled their sickening
sweet pulp until it dripped down
to form the Lake of Hali below.

That sobering city,
where twin moons flanked
folding reflections
as their shapes rippled
across Hali’s surface,
until the skyline collapsed
inward into the cosmic dusk.

For some time I was sure
I must be trapped there,
in that city
that never wakes,
whose jagged towers pointed
like frail fingers up
toward Hyades and across
forgotten galaxies.

I lost part of myself
there, in Carcosa,
where the shadows
lengthened into days,
and the sultry air
turned every living thing
into stone.



The Vortex of Gold Hill

Donielle Carr

A spherical field of force
creates a whirling mass,
the basic form of our universe.

Where the ground was once forbidden,
a shack now sits, dubbed:
The House of Mystery.

A door was drawn,
dimensions entered with exactness
though one experiences a warped perception,

like light refractions
that bend one’s sight
to create a slanted impression

that causes
fluctuations in height
and degree,

as posture proposes an inclination
drawn towards magnetic north,
creating optical illusions

that impress and amaze.
This scientific disturbance
is an excursionist’s dream.

What the Air Makes Clear

Grace Watson

Wind mixes the dirts
and awakens the trees.
The world unfolds in spurts;
a breeze attempting to steal the leaves.

Air strives to leave the atmosphere.
A silent exodus
makes clear
the emptiness in us.

Music of crickets
leaves us in admiration.
The apathy of our essence it exhibits,
when our only link to creation is flirtation.

Disconnected from the source,
it’s no surprise we lost our course.



Zombie

Donielle Carr

She thrust her fist through the Earth.
Departed no more, she is an entity
at once lost to the grave,
now animated through creation.
Mortality reaped by vitality.

She crawls across the dew-dripped grass.
A being of demise
recently deceased,
yet pulsating palpably.
Life after afterlife.

She shambles upon a wrought-iron archway.
The end begins
a new existence,
fatality a fleeting moment.
Quietus deafened by quintessence.

She moans vigorously.
A monster in the night.
Lost between the living and the dead.
